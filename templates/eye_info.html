Section 01
Diabetic Retinopathy
A diabetes complication that damages blood vessels in the retina, leading to vision loss if untreated.
What is Diabetic Retinopathy?
High blood sugar damages the tiny vessels supplying the retina. They leak, swell, or grow abnormally — potentially causing blindness. It is the leading cause of preventable blindness among working-age adults worldwide. Early detection through regular screening is the most effective intervention.

Disease Progression · 5 Stages
🟢
No DR
No visible vessel changes in the retina.
🔵
Mild NPDR
Microaneurysms — tiny swellings in vessels.
🟡
Moderate NPDR
More blocked vessels; visible retinal changes.
🟠
Severe NPDR
Many blocked vessels; retina deprived of blood.
🔴
PDR
Fragile new vessels grow — high risk of blindness.
Symptoms
  • Blurred or fluctuating vision
  • Dark strings or spots floating in vision (floaters)
  • Dark or empty areas in visual field
  • Difficulty with colour perception
  • Vision loss in advanced stages
  • No symptoms in early stages — screening is critical
Risk Factors
🩸
Poor Sugar Control
HbA1c above 7% significantly increases risk.
⏳
Duration of Diabetes
Longer duration = higher cumulative risk.
💊
High Blood Pressure
Hypertension accelerates vessel damage.
🚬
Smoking
Reduces retinal blood flow significantly.
🧬
Genetics
Family history raises susceptibility.
⚖️
High Cholesterol
Contributes to retinal vessel blockage.
Treatment Options
🔬 Early / Mild
  • Tight blood sugar control
  • Blood pressure management
  • Regular ophthalmology follow-up
  • Lifestyle modifications
💉 Moderate / Severe
  • Anti-VEGF injections
  • Intravitreal steroids
  • Focal / grid laser treatment
  • Macular oedema management
🏥 Advanced / PDR
  • Panretinal photocoagulation
  • Vitrectomy surgery
  • Retinal detachment repair
  • Ongoing specialist monitoring
Section 02
Eye Anatomy & Daily Care
Understanding your eye's structure and how to protect your vision for life.
Key Structures
Retina
Light-sensitive layer converting images to nerve signals sent to the brain.
Cornea
Clear front surface that focuses incoming light into the eye.
Lens
Flexible structure behind the iris that fine-tunes focus.
Optic Nerve
Carries visual signals from retina to the brain.
Macula
Central retinal area responsible for sharp detailed vision.
Daily Eye Care Tips
☀️
UV Protection
Wear UV400 sunglasses every time you go outdoors.
💻
20-20-20 Rule
Every 20 min, look 20 ft away for 20 seconds.
💧
Stay Hydrated
Proper hydration maintains healthy tear film.
🥗
Eat for Eyes
Leafy greens, omega-3 fish, and colourful veg.
🚭
Quit Smoking
Smoking doubles AMD and cataract risk.
📅
Annual Screening
Dilated eye exams catch silent disease early.
😴
Quality Sleep
7–8 hrs allows eyes to repair and reduce strain.
🩺
Manage Diabetes
Tight blood sugar control protects against DR.
Nutrition for Eye Health
🥬
Lutein & Zeaxanthin
Protect the macula from blue-light damage; reduce AMD risk.
Kale, spinach, eggs, broccoli
🐟
Omega-3 Fatty Acids
Support retinal cell membranes; reduce dry eye risk.
Salmon, sardines, flaxseed, walnuts
🥕
Vitamin A
Essential for low-light vision; deficiency causes night blindness.
Carrots, sweet potato, liver
🍊
Vitamin C
Antioxidant that protects the lens from cataracts.
Oranges, amla, guava, peppers
🌻
Vitamin E
Protects eye cells from free radical damage.
Sunflower seeds, almonds, hazelnuts
🦪

Helps Vitamin A produce melanin; protects retina.
Oysters, beef, pumpkin seeds, legumes
Section 03
Common Eye Diseases
Overview of prevalent eye conditions, their symptoms, and treatment approaches.
👁
Glaucoma
ChronicAffects 80M+ worldwide — silent thief of sight
What it is

Damage to the optic nerve, usually from high intraocular pressure. Progresses silently with no early symptoms until significant vision loss has occurred.

Symptoms
  • No symptoms early (open-angle)
  • Gradual peripheral vision loss
  • Tunnel vision in advanced stages
  • Sudden pain (acute angle closure)
  • Halos around lights
  • Nausea with severe headache
Treatment
  • Eye drops to reduce pressure
  • Oral medications
  • Laser therapy
  • Surgical drainage
  • Lifelong monitoring needed
  • Early detection is critical
🌫️
Cataracts
Very CommonLeading cause of reversible blindness — affects 95M+ globally
What it is

Clouding of the eye's natural lens. Develops slowly, initially without impact. Completely treatable with surgery — the most effective eye operation in medicine.

Symptoms
  • Cloudy or dim vision
  • Difficulty seeing at night
  • Sensitivity to light and glare
  • Halos around lights
  • Fading or yellowing of colours
  • Double vision in one eye
Treatment
  • Early: updated glasses
  • Surgery: phacoemulsification
  • Intraocular lens (IOL) implant
  • Monofocal / multifocal IOLs
  • Recovery within 8 weeks
  • No proven preventive medication
🔍
Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD)
Age-RelatedLeading cause of vision loss in over-50s
What it is

Deterioration of the macula — the retinal area for sharp central vision. Dry AMD is slower; wet AMD involves abnormal vessel growth and progresses faster.

Symptoms
  • Blurry central vision
  • Straight lines appear wavy
  • Dark areas in central vision
  • Difficulty reading in dim light
  • Colours less vivid
  • Gradual or sudden loss
Treatment
  • Dry AMD: AREDS2 supplements
  • Wet AMD: Anti-VEGF injections
  • Photodynamic therapy
  • Low vision aids
  • Lifestyle: quit smoking, eat greens
  • Management-focused — no cure
💧
Dry Eye Syndrome
Very CommonAffects 1 in 5 adults — rising with screen use
What it is

Insufficient tears or excessive evaporation causes inflammation and surface damage. Increasingly common due to screens, AC, and contact lens wear.

Symptoms
  • Stinging or burning sensation
  • Stringy mucus in or around eyes
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Watery eyes (reflex tearing)
  • Blurred vision, eye fatigue
  • Contact lens discomfort
Treatment
  • Artificial tear eye drops
  • Prescription drops (Cyclosporine)
  • Warm compresses on eyelids
  • Omega-3 supplements
  • Punctal plugs for severe cases
  • Reduce screen time, humidifier
⚠️ Seek Emergency Eye Care Immediately If You Experience:
These symptoms may indicate a sight-threatening emergency. Do not wait for a scheduled appointment.
Sudden vision loss Flashes of light Shower of floaters Eye injury or trauma Severe eye pain Curtain over vision Chemical in eye
